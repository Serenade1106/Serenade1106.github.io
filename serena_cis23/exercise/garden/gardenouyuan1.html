The Couple's Retreat Garden


The Couple's Retreat Garden, also known as Ouyuan Garden, is one of the most famous classical gardens in Suzhou, China. It was built during the Ming Dynasty in the 16th century and was originally a private garden for a wealthy government official. The garden has since changed hands multiple times and was renamed "Couple's Retreat Garden" during the Qing Dynasty.

The garden covers an area of about 0.6 hectares and is divided into three main sections: the eastern, central, and western sections. The eastern section is the entrance to the garden and features a small courtyard with a rockery and several pavilions. The central section is the main part of the garden and is characterized by its water features, bridges, and rock formations. The western section is the most peaceful and secluded part of the garden and contains several buildings and courtyards.

The Couple's Retreat Garden is known for its many picturesque features, including a variety of rock formations, winding streams and ponds, and beautifully manicured plants and trees. One of the most famous features of the garden is the "Flying Rock", a large rock perched on the side of a cliff that appears to be flying through the air.

Another notable feature of the garden is the "Lotus Flower Cave", a small grotto that is said to have been carved from living rock. The cave contains several inscriptions and poems, as well as a small pool filled with lotus flowers.



苏州偶园是一座中国传统园林，位于江苏省苏州市姑苏区东北角，是中国著名的园林之一。偶园是明朝嘉靖年间建造的私家园林，后数易主，曾被命名为“偶锦园”，园内有许多典雅的假山、曲水流觞、花木扶疏、错落有致的廊亭和精美的石雕等景观，是中国古典园林艺术的代表之一。

偶园分为前、中、后三个部分，园内布局精巧，景致瑰丽，石雕和园林景观完美融合。前园是主要景区，有湖泊、假山、雕塑和亭台，其中以假山为主要特色，有“飞来石”、“倒插云”等著名的景点。中园是偶园的主体，包括了“怡春院”、“醉月楼”、“迎春亭”、“抱琴亭”等景点，其中最著名的是“三笑亭”，这是一座三层楼阁，园林中的标志性建筑。后园则是偶园的私人住宅区，是一片安静祥和的景区，有园主的居所、宅院和小花园。

偶园的石雕艺术是其最大的特色之一，园内有数百块精雕细琢的石刻，题材包括人物、花鸟、山水等，其中最著名的是“石牌坊”、“玉篆亭”、“石马”等。
