The Surging Waves Pavillion


Canglang Pavilion is a famous classical garden in Suzhou, China, located on the northeast side of Shantang Street, bordering Shantang River. It is also known as the "Blue Wave Pavilion" and is considered one of the top four gardens in Suzhou, along with the Humble Administrator's Garden, Lion Grove Garden, and Lingering Garden.

The garden was first built during the Northern Song Dynasty (960-1127), and it was later renovated and expanded during the Ming (1368-1644) and Qing (1644-1911) Dynasties. The pavilion got its name from the nearby Canglang River, which means "blue waves" in Chinese.

The garden covers an area of 1.1 hectares and features a unique layout with a central pond surrounded by pavilions, corridors, rockeries, and other garden features. The garden also includes several small buildings, such as the Wufeng Tower, Jianyin Hall, and Yilan Hall, which are connected by winding paths and bridges.

The pavilion is known for its beautiful scenery, elegant architecture, and cultural significance. It has inspired many poets, artists, and scholars throughout history, and it is considered a masterpiece of Chinese classical garden design. The garden has also been recognized as a UNESCO World Heritage Site and a National AAAA Tourist Attraction in China.

Visitors can enjoy the serene atmosphere and natural beauty of the garden while exploring its many features, such as the 36 Yamen Stones, the "Listening to Orioles" Hall, and the "Sunset Glow over the Leaning Tower" rockery.



沧浪亭，位于中国江苏省苏州市城内沧浪街东南，是中国著名的古典园林之一，也是苏州古典园林的代表之一。沧浪亭始建于北宋，经历了多次扩建和修葺，成为了一座以名胜古迹、艺术品和园林景观为一体的综合性文化遗产。沧浪亭被誉为“吴中第一名胜”，被列入中国四大名亭之一，也是中国历史文化名城苏州的象征。

沧浪亭占地面积约5.2万平方米，园内建筑布局合理，风景秀丽，有“三台一榭五亭六阁”之说。园内建筑采用了传统的中国古典园林造园技巧，有着精美的石雕、木雕、砖雕等园林艺术品，也有许多古典名人题刻和文物古迹。园内还有多个小桥流水、亭台楼阁和假山石径，构成了独具特色的园林景观。沧浪亭的典型代表作品有“白莲花庵”、“曲桥流水”、“三台亭”、“缥帙亭”等。

沧浪亭在中国园林历史上占有重要的地位，是中国古典园林文化的代表之一。在1981年，沧浪亭被联合国教科文组织列入“世界文化遗产名录”，成为了苏州城内不可缺少的一部分。如今，沧浪亭是苏州市最重要的旅游景点之一，每年吸引了众多国内外游客前来参观、观赏。
